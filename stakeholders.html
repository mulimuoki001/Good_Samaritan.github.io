Our Esteemed Stakeholders

OUR STAKEHOLDERS

Good Samaritan stakeholders encompass a diverse range of entities and individuals committed to fostering altruism and community service. At the heart of this concept are the individuals and volunteers who selflessly extend a helping hand to those in need. Nonprofit organizations play a crucial role by organizing and coordinating volunteer efforts, providing essential resources, training, and support. Religious and faith-based organizations, community groups, and educational institutions contribute to nurturing a culture of compassion and empathy.

Volunteers

Volunteers are the unsung heroes who dedicate their time and skills to make a positive impact on communities and causes.

Charitable Organisations

Charitable organizations are an integral part of the Good Samaritan movement.

"Charitable homes" typically refer to residential facilities or institutions that operate with a primary focus on providing care, support, and assistance to individuals in need. These homes are often established and run by charitable organizations, nonprofit groups, or religious entities.

Partnerships and Donors

Partners and donors are individuals who have pledged their time and resources to help those in need.Partnerships and donors are pivotal pillars in fostering the success of organizations and initiatives.

Key Supporters

The Good Samaritan Project has garnered widespread support from diverse stakeholders, fostering a robust collaborative effort to address hunger and food waste.

The Rwandan Government

The Rwandan government is committed to fighting hunger and protecting the environment. The government's involvement in providing logistical support is crucial for the efficient and organized transportation of surplus food from hotels and restaurants to the intended beneficiaries. This support may include access to transportation networks, storage facilities, and coordination mechanisms that optimize the logistics of food distribution.

World Health Organisation

The World Health Organization (WHO) plays a pivotal role by ensuring the quality and safety of the donated food. Their expertise in health and safety standards is instrumental in safeguarding the well-being of recipients. The WHO likely conducts rigorous assessments and inspections to guarantee that the food donations meet the necessary health and nutritional standards, ensuring that vulnerable populations receive not only adequate but also safe and nutritious sustenance.

Non-Government Organisations

Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) are instrumental in the distribution phase of the project. Leveraging their networks and expertise in community outreach, NGOs contribute to the effective and equitable dissemination of the donated food to children's homes and charitable organizations. Their involvement ensures that the food reaches the intended beneficiaries in a timely and organized manner, maximizing the positive impact of the Good Samaritan Project on communities in need.
