Get To Know Us

Our Story

"Good Samaritan Foundation" is a creative project to close the gap between excess food and the needs of vulnerable people in Kigali, where two million tons of food go to waste every year. In Rwanda-Kigali, where restaurants and hotels contribute to excess garbage and humanitarian groups struggle with little funding, the project addresses the widespread global issue of food waste and hunger. The project's main objective is to establish a sustainable system that effectively gathers excess food and distributes it to those in need while keeping a close eye on environmental preservation. The project's strategic planning acknowledges and includes the logistical issues of distribution, transportation, and food safety standards. Beyond the complexities of logistics, the project expects to significantly influence the health of marginalised groups, especially children, by providing appropriate and nutritional meals.

As the project's focal point, Kigali is strategically chosen for its concentration of resources and needs. "Good Samaritan Rwanda" introduces a creative online platform designed as a centralised hub, facilitating the efficient collection, coordination, and redistribution of surplus food. The website's key features include a comprehensive database, enabling the tailored matching of excess food with the specific dietary needs of charitable institutions. Participating restaurants and hotels will register on the platform, detailing excess availability, cuisine type, and preferred donation schedules. The platform fosters communication and feedback between donors and recipients, ensuring a cohesive and impactful donation process.

The Good Samaritan Foundation is a transformative initiative dedicated to addressing hunger and environmental sustainability by establishing a vital link between surplus food from hotels and restaurants and those in need, including children's homes and charitable organizations. This innovative project not only tackles the pressing issue of food insecurity by redirecting unused resources to vulnerable populations but also contributes significantly to environmental conservation by preventing the decomposition of excess food waste. Through a collaborative effort between the hospitality industry and charitable organizations, the Good Samaritan Project exemplifies a community-driven approach, emphasizing the importance of shared responsibility in combating social challenges and promoting sustainable practices. This endeavor stands as a beacon of compassion, efficiently channeling surplus resources to nourish the underserved while actively participating in the global effort to reduce food waste and its environmental repercussions.

Problem Statement

In Kigali, excessive food waste coexists, with a significant portion of the population facing hunger. Surplus food from restaurants and hotels goes unused, contributing to environmental pollution. Charitable organizations struggle to meet the dietary needs of the underprivileged. The Good Samaritan Project aims to address this challenge by efficiently redistributing surplus food, reducing waste, and promoting environmental conservation.We will create an app which will be the hub for connecting all the stakeholders.

Our Mission

At Good Samaritan, our mission is to bridge the gap between abundance and need, fostering a world where no one goes hungry and where environmental sustainability is paramount. We are dedicated to addressing the dual challenges of food waste and hunger by partnering with hotels and restaurants to redistribute surplus food to those in need.

Our Vision

At Good Samaritan, we envision a world where every plate tells a story of compassion, sustainability, and shared abundance. Our vision is rooted in the belief that no person should go to bed hungry while edible food goes to waste. We aspire to be the driving force behind a global movement that transforms surplus into sustenance, creating a future where environmental responsibility and social impact converge harmoniously.
