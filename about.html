Who are We?

The Good Samaritan Foundation is a transformative initiative dedicated to addressing hunger and environmental sustainability by establishing a vital link between surplus food from hotels and restaurants and those in need, including children's homes and charitable organizations. This innovative project not only tackles the pressing issue of food insecurity by redirecting unused resources to vulnerable populations but also contributes significantly to environmental conservation by preventing the decomposition of excess food waste. Through a collaborative effort between the hospitality industry and charitable organizations, the Good Samaritan Project exemplifies a community-driven approach, emphasizing the importance of shared responsibility in combating social challenges and promoting sustainable practices. This endeavor stands as a beacon of compassion, efficiently channeling surplus resources to nourish the underserved while actively participating in the global effort to reduce food waste and its environmental repercussions.
